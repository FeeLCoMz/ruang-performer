## ğŸ¼ **2. Voicing Options**

Every chord has **4 professional voicings**:

### **Root Position**

- Standard full chord: `G - B - D`
- Most recognizable, clear sound
- Best for: Pop, rock, contemporary music

### **Shell Voicing (Jazz)**

- Simplified to Root, 3rd, 7th: `G - B - F#`
- Elegant, spacious sound
- Perfect for: Jazz, ballads, sophisticated comping
- Uses less notes = easier to comp quickly

### **Left Hand Bass**

- Bass in left hand, chord in right
- Shows both hands separately
- **Left Hand:** Bass note (with sustain pedal indicator ğŸš—)
- **Right Hand:** Full chord voicing
- Best for: Classical approach, clear separation

### **Upper Structure**

- Inverted high voicing: `D - B - G`
- Bright, modern sound
- Best for: Contemporary music, rich textures

## ğŸ’¡ **8. Keyboardist Tips & Best Practices**

### **For Different Styles**

**Jazz & Ballads**

- Use **Shell Voicing Mode** for spacious, elegant sound
- Play walking bass with left hand
- Activate sustain for smooth transitions

**Pop & Rock**

- Use **Root Position** for clear, full sound
- Keep voicings in comfortable middle range (C4-C5)
- Practice clean hand position transitions

**Contemporary Music**

- Mix voicings: Root position for verses, shell for chorus
- Use **Upper Structure** for bright, modern sound
- Layer with both hands for richer texture

1. Click on **ANY chord** to see **4 voicing options**
2. Try each one - **see which sounds best**

4. **Shell Mode** is great for quick learning

### **Performance Tips**

- Use **Keyboardist Mode** at gigs for clarity
- Memorize shell voicings for quick changes
- Practice hand position transitions
- Use sustain pedal strategically to avoid muddiness
